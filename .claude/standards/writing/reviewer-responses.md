# Writing voice — responses to reviewers

Apply `@~/.claude/standards/writing/CORE.md`. Tone shift for this genre: diplomatic and
deferential, even when the reviewer is wrong.

## Default move: thank, take responsibility, then cite the evidence
> "We thank the reviewer for raising this point. We may not have explained it clearly: as shown in Fig. 3, the effect persists after controlling for X."

## Rules
- Open each reply by thanking the reviewer for the specific point.
- When you disagree, assume the gap is in your explanation, not their reading. Soften, then point to the figure or data that settles it.
- Stay concise and concrete: cite specific figures, lines, or new analyses.
- Never sound defensive or curt. Let the evidence do the arguing.
- Keep the core habits: active voice, short sentences, numbers inline, no dashes.
