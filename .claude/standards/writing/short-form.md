# Writing voice — short-form (email, talks, slide text)

Apply `@~/.claude/standards/writing/CORE.md`. Register: **neutral-professional**. Friendly
and brisk, but no slang and no contractions, and no formal throat-clearing.

## Email
> "The new data look strong (R² = 0.9). Could you send the updated figures by Friday?"
- Get to the point in sentence one. Numbers inline. One clear ask.
- Avoid "I am writing to…" openers. Avoid "Hey, quick one" casualness. Land in the middle.

## Talks / slides
- Short declarative lines, staccato rhythm. One idea per line.
- Plain words; gloss any term the audience may not know.
- Numbers carry the point; cut adjectives.
