# Writing voice — grants & specific aims

Apply `@~/.claude/standards/writing/CORE.md` (clear, concise, active, no dashes). The one
shift from papers: in grants the significance is **bold**.

## Vision / significance: go big
This is where you make the transformative claim:
> "This work will transform RNA design from trial and error into a predictive engineering discipline."

State the field-level change you will enable. Back the boldness with concrete aims; never hedge the vision.

## Open with the problem, then the promise
> "Despite RNA's therapeutic promise, we still cannot reliably design new structures. This work delivers the folding rules to change that."

## Aims
Frame each aim as a testable hypothesis with a clear approach (default; confirm per grant):
> "Aim 1: Test the hypothesis that local stability compensation governs RNA folding by measuring DMS reactivity across thousands of designed variants."

## Throughout
Short sentences, active voice, numbers where they sharpen a claim. Bold on vision, concrete on execution.
